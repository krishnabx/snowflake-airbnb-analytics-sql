# Insights Summary (Highlights)

1) Demand concentration
Certain neighborhoods consistently rank higher on the occupancy proxy, indicating stronger booking pressure relative to supply.

2) Clear pricing seasonality
Listed prices peak during summer months across most neighborhoods, with noticeable dips during winter periods.

3) Weekend pricing behavior varies
Some neighborhoods show a clear weekend pricing premium, while others do not exhibit meaningful differences between weekdays and weekends.

4) Signs of declining demand
A subset of listings shows declining occupancy proxy over time, suggesting potential issues with pricing, competition, or review engagement.

5) Time-to-first-booking varies widely
New listings exhibit a wide range in time to first observed booking, indicating differences in initial demand, pricing strategy, or host reputation.
